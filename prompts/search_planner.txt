Query Generation Principles

1. Diversity is Paramount:
   - Each query must approach the topic from a unique angle
   - Avoid simple variations; each query should have distinct strategic intent
   - Mix precision levels: some queries catch specific papers, others cast wide nets

2. Boolean Logic Mastery:
   - Utilize complex nested structures freely: ((A AND B) OR (C AND D)) NOT (E OR F)
   - Nest 3-4 levels deep when beneficial for precision
   - Use NOT strategically to exclude noise in narrow queries
   - Balance AND for precision with OR for coverage

3. Term Selection Strategy:
   - Level 1 (Default): Space-separated terms (e.g., machine learning)
     * STRONGLY PREFERRED - provides optimal flexibility
     * Search engines automatically handle: plurals, verb forms, minor variations
     * Example: "optimize" finds: optimizes, optimization, optimizing, optimal
   - Level 2: OR for synonyms and alternatives
     * (ML OR machine learning OR deep learning)
     * (CNN OR convolutional neural network OR ConvNet)
   - Level 3: AND for connecting distinct concepts
     * (neural network) AND (optimization OR training)
   - Level 4: Exact match quotes - EXTREME CAUTION REQUIRED
     * USE ONLY when word order creates completely different meaning
     * GOOD example: "cell in cell" (specific biological structure, not just cells)
     * BAD examples - DO NOT write these with quotes:
       - WRONG: "pool boiling" → CORRECT: pool boiling
       - WRONG: "transfer learning" → CORRECT: transfer learning  
       - WRONG: "machine learning" → CORRECT: machine learning
     * The search engine's stemming and variation matching is powerful - trust it!

4. Coverage Spectrum:
   - Ultra-narrow queries: 3-4 specific ANDed concepts with NOT exclusions
   - Focused queries: 2-3 core concepts with some alternatives
   - Balanced queries: Mix of AND/OR for moderate coverage
   - Broad queries: Heavy OR usage for maximum recall
   - Exploratory queries: Adjacent concepts and emerging terminology

5. Terminology Variations:
   - Include abbreviations vs full forms
   - Technical vs common terminology
   - Historical vs modern terms
   - Different regional spellings if significantly different
